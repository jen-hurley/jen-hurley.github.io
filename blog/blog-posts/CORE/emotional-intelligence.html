Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

The ability to control and express your own emotions, as well as the ability to understand, interpret and respond to others emotions.

The 4 levels of emotional intelligence:

- Percieving Emotions: To understand emotions you need to first be able to percieve them accurately. This involves understanding non-verbals signs such as body language and facial expressions.

- Reasoning with emotions: You can then use emotions to promote thinking and cognitive activy. Use the emotions to help prioritize what you pay attention and react to.

- Understanding emotions: The emotions that are perceived can have a variety of meanings. If someone is expressing a particular emotion, the observer must interpret the cause and what it could mean. e.g. someone is acting angrily: are they angry at you for something you did, or are they angry because they're having a shitty day?

- Managing emotions: The ability to manage emotions effectively is crucial and the highest level of emotional intelligence. This means to regulate and respond appropriately to your own emotions, as well as appropriately respond to the emotions of others.

Examples of Emotional Intelligence:

- Identify and describe your own emotions, and also the emotions of others.

- Awareness of personal strengths and limitations

- Self-confidence and self-acceptance

- The ability to let go of mistakes (forgiveness)

- The ability to accept and embrace change (flexability)

- A strong sense of curiosity, particularly curiosity about other people

- Feelings of empathy and concern for others

- Showing sensitivity to the feelings of other people

- Accepting responsibility for your own mistakes

- The ability to manage emotions, even in situations that are difficult

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ is the ability to recognize and respond to your own emotions and the emotions of others.

Whereas IQ is the ability to understand (and communicate) complex ideas, solve problems and use logic.

Why is EQ important?

Your emotions are linked directly to how you communicate with others.

By being able to empathize with others, communicate effectively, and by being socially and self aware it helps build and strength interpersonal relationships.

It also helps with self-confidence and self-acceptance by being able to non-judementally notice your own emotions, express them, and manage them appropriately. It improves your self-control which allows you to stay calm under pressure and handle situations better.

Being able to mangage, understand your own emotions and the emotions of other people helps you to interact and communicate with other people and yourself in a healthy, appropriate and non-confrontational way.
